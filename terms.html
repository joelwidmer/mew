    Eligible Organizations

    The program is open to public schools located in the fifty (50) United States and the District of Columbia that provide classes to enrolled students in one or more grades from kindergarten through grade 12. The program is also open to non-profit organizations, located in the fifty (50) United States and the District of Columbia, and recognized as 501(c)(3) charitable organizations by the U.S. Internal Revenue Service and which have a mission to benefit the local community. A mission to benefit the local community could include, but is not limited to:

    • Providing relief to the poor
    • Advancing education
    • Improving social welfare
    • Preserving culture
    • Preserving or restoring the environment
    • Promoting human rights
    • Preventing acts of violence

    IGA reserves the right to determine eligibility in its sole discretion, and may deny participation in the program at any time for failure to meet eligibility requirements. IGA may request eligibility documentation at any time including, but not limited to, IRS determination letters of tax exempt status from participating organizations.

    Ineligible Organizations

    The following organizations are ineligible for the program:

    • Nonprofit organizations that have not obtained recognized charitable status
    • Governmental organizations or agencies, except for eligible U.S. public schools;
    • Commerce and Trade Associations without charitable aims or activities benefitting non-members;
    • Sponsorships of events, tables, exhibitions, or performances;
    • Fund-raising events such as luncheons, dinners, walks, runs, or sports tournaments;
    • Political, Labor, and Fraternal organizations; and
    • Individuals.

    How do organizations enroll in the program?

    Organizations are required to fill out a registration card and submit it to IGA through this website or mail it to the address on the registration form. A copy of the organization’s W-9 form must be included with the first bundle of original UPC labels from eligible products to complete the registration.

    Redemption

    Collect eligible UPC labels and given them to the program administrator of your organization. Each bundle of 500 eligible UPC labels is worth $50.00, effective October 1, 2014. The program administrator must bundle the UPC labels in groups of 500 and send them to: Attn: Hometown Label Savers, IGA USA, 8745 W. Higgins Rd., Suite 350, Chicago, IL 60631 to redeem them for cash. Each bundle must have a completed label redemption form attached to it. UPC labels are not otherwise redeemable for cash and have no cash value.

    We recommend selecting a method of mailing which provides you with a receipt of delivery, such as Certified Mail. IGA is not responsible for lost, stolen or misdirected mail or shipments of UPC labels. All mailing and shipping costs are the responsibility of the participating organization.

    Which products are eligible?

    All IGA Brand products sold in IGA stores located in the fifty (50) United States and the District of Columbia are eligible except for gift cards, products packaged in aluminum, store-made products, fresh meats and products without a UPC code beginning in 041270.

    Program Restrictions

    Original UPC labels from eligible products may be submitted only if they were clipped from original product packaging by the final purchaser of the product. All other UPC labels are not permitted and will be considered invalid submissions in IGA’s sole discretion. UPC labels that contain invalid or expired codes or are missing codes are invalid. UPC labels that have been altered, reproduced, defaced, mutilated, or forged are invalid. All submissions are subject to verification by IGA, which shall be in IGA’s sole discretion. Any other part of the product packaging is not a substitute for an original UPC label.

    Payments

    Payments will be made to organizations on a monthly basis. IGA will normally process payments during the month following IGA’s receipt of the eligible UPC labels, but in some cases payment may be delayed. All redemption checks must be cashed within twelve (12) months of receipt or the organization will no longer be considered eligible for the program and will forfeit any payment. IGA will not replace checks requested more than twelve (12) months after issuance. A valid proof of delivery and valid copies of label redemption forms will be required in the event of a payment discrepancy or dispute.

    Maximum refund amount

    An organization may not receive refunds totaling more than $15,000.
